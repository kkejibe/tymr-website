Where the numbers come from

We use the Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC) method at the federal level — 14% of current-year Qualified Research Expenses above 50% of the average prior 3-year QREs. For a typical software org with stable or growing R&D, that comes out to roughly 7–10% of current QRE as a federal credit. We default to 8% as a balanced middle.

State credits are calibrated against published 2024–2025 state R&D credit surveys (BDO, EY, Deloitte, KBKG). Each state percentage shown above represents the effective rate as a percentage of total QRE for a typical software company that's been claiming for 3+ years — accounting for incremental vs non-incremental methods, base-period rules, refundability, and statewide caps.

What counts as QRE? Wages for employees performing qualified research, supplies consumed in R&D, 65% of contract research, and (post-TCJA / Section 174) certain capitalized SRE expenditures. Management and back-office time generally doesn't qualify.

Why the substantiation matters more than the math

The math above is the easy part. The hard part — and the reason most companies under-claim — is substantiation: proving to the IRS that the work actually qualified, with contemporaneous evidence tied to specific projects and engineers.

That's what Tymr produces. Every hour in your R&D credit worksheet traces back to a Jira ticket transition, a GitHub pull request SHA, or a calendar block — time-stamped and immutable. When your tax firm prepares Form 6765 or your company faces a § 41 exam, the evidence is already on file.

Where the ROI comes from

Manual time tracking has four leaks. Tymr seals each one with a different mechanism.

Captured hours

Every Jira transition, GitHub commit, and calendar block becomes an event — recovering ~50% of the hours timesheets miss. For billable teams, recovered hours convert at full rate.

Admin time

Auto-aggregation eliminates 1–3 hours/week per user of timesheet entry. Compounded across the team, that's a full FTE recovered per ~25 users.

Allocation accuracy

Real per-engineer, per-project hours surface where effort actually went. PM and ops use the data to rebalance staffing — the typical productivity lift is 4–7%.

How the four leaks compound

The biggest lever depends on your shop. Agencies and services firms see the largest captured-hours uplift (every recovered hour is billable). Internal software teams see the largest allocation lift (visibility drives reprioritization). Capitalized-product teams see the biggest finance lift (audit-ready CapEx evidence).
