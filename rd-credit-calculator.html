Where the numbers come from

We use the Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC) method — 14% of current-year Qualified Research Expenses above 50% of the average prior 3-year QREs. For a typical software org without a declining R&D trend, that comes out to roughly 7–10% of current QRE as a federal credit. We default to 8% to stay conservative.

State credits vary widely. California adds ~7.5% effective for qualifying wages; Texas offers a 5% franchise-tax credit; Washington has no state R&D credit. The dropdown above uses rough effective rates — your preparer will apply the actual state formula.

What counts as QRE? Wages for employees performing qualified research, supplies consumed in R&D, and 65% of contract research. Management and back-office time generally doesn't qualify.

Why the substantiation matters more than the math

The math above is the easy part. The hard part — and the reason most companies under-claim — is substantiation: proving to the IRS that the work actually qualified, with contemporaneous evidence tied to specific projects and engineers.
