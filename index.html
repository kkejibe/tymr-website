Where Tymr sits

Tymr isn't a time tracker. It's a workforce intelligence platform with a compliance layer.

There's a wide gap between consumer time trackers (Toggl, Harvest, Clockify) and enterprise workforce-management suites (Replicon, Deltek, Workday). Tymr occupies that middle stratum — but with four capabilities no competitor at any tier currently offers. That's the first-mover window.

1. AI agent tracking. No time tracking product currently sees AI agent sessions, capacity, or project allocation alongside human workers. As enterprises scale agent deployment, they need exactly this — mixed-workforce capacity planning, cost attribution between human and agent labor, and project-level AI utilization. Tymr owns this lane right now.

2. The Virtual Floor. Real-time presence, room operations, meeting cost ticker, and the time ledger in one canvas. Solves remote-team visibility in a way no time tracker attempts — and the live $/hr burn during P1 / P2 incidents is genuinely novel.

3. SOX CapEx + R&D Credit PDF exports. High-stakes, audit-mandatory outputs that justify per-seat spend on their own. A finance team defending an R&D credit claim or capitalization schedule pays a premium just for these. No direct competitor generates them natively.

4. EVM-native project health (SPI / CPI). Earned Value Management is normally only in MS Project or Primavera. Having it inside the time layer — connected to logged hours, rate cards, and budgets — is a meaningful capability gap Tymr fills for engineering and professional services.

How it works

Three steps. No timesheets at any of them.

You don't interrupt your engineers. You don't re-platform your team. Tymr reads the tools your team already uses — and does the rest continuously.

01 · Collect

Connect Jira, GitHub, and your calendar.

OAuth setup in under an hour. Tymr reads event streams — ticket transitions, commits, meeting blocks — in real time. No desktop agent, no browser plugin, no engineer survey.

Every hour gets tagged automatically.

Each Jira transition, commit, and calendar block is attributed to the engineer and project, then classified: R&D-qualified, software CapEx, or OpEx. Low-confidence calls go to a human for a 30-minute weekly review.

Auto-tagged · reviewable · cited to source
