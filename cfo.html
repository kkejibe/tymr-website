Who this is for

We built the pilot narrowly on purpose. It works when all three are true:

  • US or Canadian software company with 50–500 engineers where software development is material to the business.
  • Already claimed an R&D credit or SR&ED in the last 1–3 years (or plan to claim this year).
  • Jira + GitHub + Google or Microsoft Calendar — the three systems where the work actually happens.
Not a fit

Non-software companies, orgs under 50 engineers, anyone without a tax advisor on the R&D study, or teams not on Jira + GitHub. We'll say so on the first call and point you at simpler tools.

The math behind the pilot

A 100-engineer software company leaves ~$790K federal on the table — before state.

At a fully-loaded $180K/engineer and 55% qualifying R&D time, that's ~$9.9M in QREs. A conservative 8% federal ASC credit is ~$790K. California adds another ~$740K; Massachusetts ~$640K.

Most of that gets claimed. What doesn't get claimed is the audit defensibility — which is why preparers haircut aggressive claims 20–40%. Tymr's pilot removes the haircut by putting a source event behind every hour.
