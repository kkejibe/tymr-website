How does the R&D credit payback math work?

A 100-engineer software company with a fully-loaded dev cost of $180K/engineer spends roughly $18M/year on engineering. If 55% of that work qualifies for the US R&D credit (typical for product-shipping software companies), that's ~$9.9M in Qualified Research Expenses. At a conservative 8% federal ASC rate, that's ~$790K in federal credit alone — before state add-ons (California adds another ~$740K, Massachusetts ~$640K).

Tymr's Growth tier for that org costs ~$14K/year. Even at the low end — federal only, no state — that's a ~55× return on the platform cost. And that's before counting auditor hours saved on SOX capitalization or under-claimed credits from prior years (open years can often be amended).
