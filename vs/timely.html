TYMR VS TIMELY

Timely watches the laptop. Tymr reads the source systems.

Timely captures every app, doc, and meeting on each user's computer so nothing slips through. Tymr ingests the systems your engineering work already runs through — Jira, GitHub, calendars — and turns that into audit-ready tax and accounting evidence. Different trust models for different buyers.

The honest summary

Timely is excellent at what it was designed for: capturing fragmented multi-client work on personal devices so nothing goes un-billed. The desktop Memory AI watches apps and suggests time entries; users review and submit. Agencies love it.

Tymr doesn't install on endpoints. No desktop tracker, no keystroke capture, no browser extension. We read the systems where engineering work is already recorded (Jira issues, GitHub PRs, calendar blocks, agent SDK events) and classify it against IRS § 174 and ASC 350-40. Different philosophy, different buyer.

When to pick which

Pick Timely if

You bill multiple clients from fragmented laptop work

  • Agency or consultancy model — hours become invoices
  • Your team works across many apps per day; memory of what happened is the hard part
  • Users trust an on-device tracker that lets them review before submitting
  • Tax credit & software capitalization aren't the reason you're tracking
Pick Tymr if

You need audit-ready R&D credit & CapEx evidence

  • You're a software / product company claiming the § 41 R&D credit
  • Your security team won't approve desktop-level monitoring of engineers
  • AI agents do meaningful engineering work alongside humans
  • You want a metadata-only trust model — no content, no keystrokes

Where engineers stand on monitoring

Endpoint-tracking tools are often resisted by engineering teams — even when the intent is billing. Tymr was designed around that constraint: we don't install on anyone's laptop, we never see code diffs by default, and every engineer has a private view of their own record with visibility controls. Classification happens on source-system metadata, not on what an individual typed.
