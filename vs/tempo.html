TYMR VS TEMPO

Tempo bills hours. Tymr substantiates R&D credit.

Tempo Timesheets is the mature Jira-native time-tracking app for client billing and project hours. Tymr is a labor-cost evidence layer for tax and audit. Both read from Jira; they're built for different buyers.

The honest summary

If your team already bills clients by the hour from Jira, Tempo works well. It's what it was built for. Engineers submit timesheets, managers approve, finance exports.

If your company claims the US R&D tax credit or capitalizes software costs under ASC 350-40, timesheets are the wrong artifact. The IRS and your auditors want contemporaneous source-event evidence — a hash-chained trail of Jira transitions, GitHub PRs, and calendar blocks tied to specific engineers and projects. Tymr produces that without timesheets.

When to pick which

Pick Tempo if

You bill clients by the hour from Jira

  • Services / agency model — contractor hours rolled to invoices
  • You need PM-approved timesheets for project management
  • You live in Jira and want the time tracker inside it
  • Tax credit & software capitalization aren't part of your CFO's workflow
Pick Tymr if

You need audit-ready R&D credit & CapEx evidence

  • You claim the US R&D tax credit (§ 41) or want to
  • You capitalize software costs under ASC 350-40 for SOX
  • Your engineers won't fill out timesheets — and you don't want them to
  • AI agents do meaningful engineering work in your org

They can run side by side

Tempo and Tymr solve different problems. If you're already on Tempo for billing, Tymr doesn't replace it — we add the tax and audit evidence layer on top of the same Jira data. Most R&D-credit customers keep their existing PM stack.
